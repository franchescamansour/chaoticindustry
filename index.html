RILEY STEWART

Riley Stewart studied English Literature and Theatre at the University of Ottawa and has worked as a playwright, actor and musician since 1987. As a playwright, he was selected to participate in the National Arts Centre “Playwright’s Circle” program and was the founder of the production company Labours of Original Thought. Riley’s credits as playwright include Memphis Liturgies, Legends and Monuments and Tales of the Redwood Forest. His credits as a musician include the Great Canadian Theatre Company production of Wendy Lill’s Sisters, Triangle Vitale’s productions of Signale D’Alarme and Les Consciences Fragile, the National Arts Centre productions of Snowlands and The Whore of Barcelona, as well as the full score and lyrics for a number of Odyssey Theatre productions, including Don Juan and The Green Bird. Riley was also composer and creator of the full-length musical Exhibit A, which was produced in association with the National Arts Centre and the Great Canadian Theatre Company. His music has also appeared in productions in Montreal, Toronto and Vancouver, and in numerous films and television series, including The Secret Garden and Sweet Tarts Takeaway. He has also provided music for a number of computer games, including Mode and Midnight Stranger. As an actor, Riley has worked with many companies from across Canada, including Odyssey Theatre, the National Arts Centre, the Great Canada Theatre Company, Tinderbox, Third Wall and Vision Theatre. His recent film and television credits include In Strict Tempo, Two’s A Mob, Juiced, Butch Patterson, Endless Grind, and a principal role in the Comedy Network series Last Band on the Planet. Riley’s vocal talents can also he heard in such animated series as Untalkative Bunny, YTV’s Freaky Stories, and Kevin Spencer. As a theatre educator, Riley has worked with a number of organizations, including Carleton University and the Ottawa Separate School Board, providing workshops in improvisation, mask and physical theatre. Riley currently lives in Ottawa.
